About Me

I am a robotics-oriented mechanical engineer with an M.Sc. background in mechatronics and robotics. I earned my B.Eng. in Mechanical Manufacturing Engineering & Automation from Shandong University and am currently an M.Sc. student in Mechanical Engineering (Mechatronics & Robotics track) at Politecnico di Milano.

My work focuses on contact-rich interaction and underactuated systems, aiming to connect model-based control (MPC, trajectory optimization, whole-body control) with learning-based methods (deep RL, imitation / representation learning) under real-world constraints such as uncertainty, friction, and latency. I value reproducible research artifacts: clear task definitions, unified baselines, and evaluation protocols.

  • Primary focus: manipulation and contact dynamics, with locomotion / body control as a complementary validation platform.
  • Method interests: MPC / MPPI / iLQR-DDP, QP-based control, deep RL (PPO-style), sim-to-real robustness.
  • Sensing interests: interaction-aware perception (contact state, tactile signals) and multimodal feedback for control.

Selected Projects

Dynamic manipulation of deformable linear objects (DLO)

Differentiable simulation and control prototyping for underactuated, contact-rich dynamics.

  • Built a GPU-accelerated simulation workflow to study elastic DLO dynamics and fast, whip-like motion primitives.
  • Designed an optimization-friendly pipeline for trajectory optimization / MPC-style rollouts and analysis.
  • Focused on practical modeling issues: stiffness/constraint tuning, contact handling, and failure modes.

Quadruped locomotion with deep reinforcement learning

Training, evaluation, and robustness testing in Isaac Gym / legged-gym style environments.

  • Set up a training pipeline for locomotion policies and standardized evaluation across terrains and disturbances.
  • Explored reward shaping, curriculum ideas, and domain randomization to improve robustness and generalization.
  • Built reproducible experiment scripts and logging to support fair comparisons across settings.
